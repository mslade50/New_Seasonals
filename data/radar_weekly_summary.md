## MP Materials — The market is still pricing a miner; the DoD made it a regulated asset

**Variant perception**: Consensus treats MP at 46x forward `+1y` EPS ($1.31) as a standard rare-earth miner subject to NdPr price cyclicality. The DoD's 15% equity stake plus a **10-year $110/kg NdPr price floor** structurally changes the cashflow profile. This isn't a commodity miner multiple problem — it's a "which asset class does MP belong to" problem. Price-floor + sole-source-of-scale + national-security offtake is closer to a defense contractor than to a miner. That reframing hasn't been priced yet.

**Who's on the other side**: Informed disagreement from rare-earth-fatigue sellers who rode MP from $18 → $100 → $61 and are anchored on commodity volatility. They point to execution risk on the magnet plant ramp and legacy China-linked offtake overhang. Both are real, but neither addresses the floor mechanic.

**The data**:
- Price $60.99, -39% from 52w high of $100.25, SMA50 recovering above SMA200
- Forward EPS: `0y` $0.27 (+213%), `+1y` $1.31 (+383%) — range $0.83–$1.95 on 10 analysts
- Last 4 surprise pattern: +163%, +38%, +35%, +1% — street has been systematically too low
- China Announcement 18 export controls on 7 heavy REEs (early April) + MP halting all concentrate exports to China on 125% tariffs — structural supply shock, not narrative
- Catalyst: Q1 print 2026-05-07

**What would change my mind**: Evidence the DoD offtake terms are materially less favorable than the headline suggests (commitment volumes lower than expected, floor with caps on upside), or magnet plant ramp slips beyond early 2026. A second leg lower in NdPr spot with MP trading at the floor price ($110/kg) for multiple quarters would compress the "utility multiple" thesis.

## TVTX — Forward valuation doesn't match the forward EPS

**Variant perception**: TVTX trades at **8.9x `+1y` forward EPS of $4.56** after the April 13 full-approval FSGS label expansion — a fundamental valuation disconnect. The street has already written the higher FILSPARI ramp into their models (hence $4.56), but the multiple hasn't re-rated. Market is treating this like every other small-cap biotech approval pop that fades. The distinction: the label expansion hit a first-in-class indication with no approved competitor and a 100k+ patient population.

**Who's on the other side**: Rotational biotech capital taking profits on the 30% one-day move, plus genuine execution skepticism — FSGS without nephrotic syndrome is a new physician conversation and commercial ramp is not automatic. There's also residual street memory of FILSPARI's prior accelerated-approval controversies.

**The data**:
- Price $40.77, -5.9% from 52w high, strong uptrend, RSI 75.6
- Forward EPS acceleration: `0y` $1.57 (+72%), `+1y` $4.56 (+191%) — not decelerating, accelerating
- Range on `+1y`: $3.49–$6.52 across 7 analysts (reasonable dispersion)
- Trailing PE n/a (losses) but last 4 surprises: -0.8%, +794%, +47%, +52% — street is systematically behind
- Catalyst: Q1 earnings 2026-04-30

**What would change my mind**: Q1 print showing script volume tracking below pre-approval trajectory (i.e., new indication not actually driving new prescribers), analyst downward revisions to `+1y` below $3.50, or reimbursement pushback from payers on the expanded label.
