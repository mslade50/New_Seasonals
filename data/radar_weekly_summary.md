## SMR (NuScale Power) — long

**Thesis.** NuScale is down 79.6% from its 52w high ($57.42 → $11.74) into the densest catalyst window the SMR vendor class has ever had. Inside the next ~3 weeks: NRC approval of the uprated design (just delivered — brief 5), the July 4 DOE pilot-criticality deadline for three reactors, and the Romania RoPower 6-module go/no-go. Sitting on top: ENTRA1+TVA up-to-6 GW US discussions, South Korea Uljin evaluation, $94M DOE supply-chain allocations awarded in May, ~$1B liquidity.

**Variant perception.** The market is pricing SMR like a dead 2024 hype cycle — the tape hasn't recovered, retail bagholders from $57 are still net sellers, the multiple has gone from "story stock" to "left for dead." What's actually true is the procurement pipeline got *deeper* across the crash: NRC certification (one of two SMR designs ever certified in the US) eliminated the biggest binary discount that institutions had been carrying, the Trump nuclear EOs framed SMR as "critical defense infrastructure with a 30-month deployment mandate," and a RoPower commitment books multi-year revenue. The 80% drawdown is a cap-table reset, not a thesis break.

**Who's on the other side.** Two cohorts. (1) 2024-peak retail buyers dollar-cost-averaging into capitulation — forced-by-pain selling, no view. (2) Institutions applying a pre-NRC-certification discount that no longer applies — the binary is resolved, the discount hasn't been re-rated out. Both seller cohorts are mechanical/stale, not informed.

**The data.** Price $11.74, RSI 45.5 (basing), -79.6% from 52w high. Forward EPS negative (-$0.51 0y / -$0.69 +1y) so multiple analysis doesn't fit — this is binary-catalyst-driven, not multiple-driven. FCF yield -3.99% on the ~$1B cash pile = ~5 years of runway at current burn before financing risk re-enters. Recovering trend (price > 50d while 50d < 200d) = early basing.

**What would change my mind.** A delayed or negative RoPower decision, or a public NRC complication that voids the certification work. Also: a sustained break of the $8.85 52w low would say the bagholder unwind isn't finished and there's more forced supply below.

## LEU (Centrus Energy) — long (paired with SMR)

**Thesis.** Centrus is the only US-licensed HALEU producer. Every advanced SMR vendor — Oklo, NuScale, X-energy, TerraPower — needs HALEU. The Russian uranium import ban ramping toward 2028 + the Urenco USA enrichment expansion + the OKLO multi-core LOI (multi-year, potential prepayments) + the Section 382 NOL-rights extension through June 2029 (a board signal that they're shielding losses for a future tax-paying business) collectively make LEU the cleanest upstream monopoly in the policy stack. SMR is the binary; LEU prints regardless of which vendor wins.

**Variant perception.** The market is treating LEU as a high-PE speculative nuclear name (trailing PE 69, forward 48) and discounting the moat — domestic HALEU licenses don't price on a multiple, they price on a contract-dollar pipeline against replacement cost of competing capacity. There is no second credible US HALEU licensee. HALEU contracts price years ahead of fuel-load dates, which means the moat shows up in revenue much later than the catalyst that creates it. Consensus is buying LEU as a fuel-cycle option; it is a fuel-cycle monopoly with first-mover capacity.

**Who's on the other side.** Generalist energy investors screening on multiple — they pass at 48x forward. They miss that the relevant framework here is contract-pipeline-against-replacement-cost, not PE. Also: nuclear bagholders from the $464 peak still working out of positions. Thin sell-side coverage (3-5 analysts) is the neglect tell — broad-mandate funds don't know how to value this.

**The data.** Price $191.39, RSI 53 (neutral), -58.8% from 52w high. Forward EPS $4.16 (0y) → $3.97 (+1y), with the +1y range spanning $1.64-$7.33 — the consensus midpoint is itself buried in contract-timing uncertainty, not earnings-power uncertainty. Recovering trend, FCF yield slightly negative (-0.29%) on a profitable trailing base. Trailing PE 69 vs forward 48 implies the street is starting to price recovery but hasn't finished.

**What would change my mind.** A second US HALEU licensee announcement (would break the monopoly — currently no credible competitor). A Russian-supply ban delay or carve-out that resets the timeline. SMR-vendor consolidation that shrinks the addressable customer set.

## MP (MP Materials) — long

**Thesis.** Only operating US rare-earth miner. Pentagon owns ~15% and has guaranteed $110/kg NdPr — that is a contract-level price floor, not a sentiment-level tailwind. China added MP to its export-control list on June 22; the structural-reroute thesis the market has talked about for two years just had its trigger pulled. Mid-2026 commissioning of a heavy rare-earth separation line at Mountain Pass extends the product mix beyond the light rare earths that have carried the multiple, into the heavy slate that China dominates >85%.

**Variant perception.** The market is pricing MP as a thematic rare-earth long with normal commodity-cycle mean-reversion risk (forward 1y EPS $1.10 implies multiple compression off the spot tape). What's structural: the DoD-guaranteed NdPr floor *removes the lower tail of the commodity cycle*, and the heavy rare-earth commissioning adds a second product line with structurally tighter supply than the light slate. The consensus +1y EPS range ($0.53-$1.72) prices commodity-cycle dispersion; the truth is the distribution should be left-truncated by the price floor and right-skewed by the heavy-line ramp. Neither shows in the midpoint.

**Who's on the other side.** Commodity-cycle traders applying historical China-driven price-drawdown probabilities that no longer apply when DoD is a backstop. Sentiment-followers who read the China sanction headline as "MP got punished" rather than "MP just got a US-policy bid mechanically locked in." Brief 1 made this point directly: "sentiment is lagging the structural setup."

**The data.** Price $60.88, RSI 44.7, -39.3% from 52w high, weakening tape (price < 50d but 50d ≈ 200d → basing). Forward EPS by period $0.04 → $0.08 → $0.24 → $1.10 — the +1y is +358% growth off a near-zero base. Trailing PE null (loss). Multiple is rich on +1y consensus (~55x); the bet is that the consensus point estimate is too low because it doesn't price the floor or the heavy-line ramp.

**What would change my mind.** Mountain Pass operational disruption (heavy line slips into 2027). Any walking-back of the DoD $110/kg floor. A US-China trade deal that includes a rare-earth carve-out — would gut the moat.
